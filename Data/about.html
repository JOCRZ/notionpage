The Journey That Led Me to Create Novel WaveHz

Published on November 20, 2025

Hi listeners!

This Journey began because one of my favorite audiobook channels on YouTube, @anyaaudiobook9931, suddenly got banned.
I used to listen to novels there every day, and when it disappeared, it genuinely affected me because audiobooks had become a big part of my life.

To understand why it mattered so much, you need to know how I ended up here.

I’ve never been a person who enjoys reading.
I’ve always preferred listening and watching.
My media journey began with cartoons, which evolved into movies, especially mind-bending films and investigation thrillers.

One day, I accidentally downloaded a TV series from Telegram. I thought it was a movie because the file was packed with episodes. Turns out, it was Stranger Things. That mistake launched my binge-watching era. I got totally hooked and devoured most of the popular shows out there.

This path unexpectedly led me to anime.
My first anime was Death Note, purely because it fit my love for psychological and investigative stories. I had zero expectations like many people,
I used to think anime was “Just cartoons”.

Death Note changed that perception.
Still, I wasn’t fully hooked until a school friend recommended Haikyuu. Giving it a chance was one of the best decisions I ever made.
I had never felt that level of excitement and happiness from any show before.

I was so excited that I literally ran to my cousin’s house just to tell her about it. Of course, she also said what everyone says: “It’s just a cartoon.”
But she listened and that meant a lot.
Happiness always grows when it’s shared.

After that, I watched anime for years. I practically abandoned other types of media.
My cousins would joke, telling me to “watch what humans watch,” but only true anime watchers understand the depth of emotions these stories give.
Some feelings can never be explained, only experienced.

As a kid, I used to watch Doraemon and Ninja Hattori without knowing they were anime.

Anime became a huge part of my life.
I caught up with the Big 3.
Naruto holds a special place in my heart.
And watching One Piece felt as if I had lived an entirely different lifetime.

I believe some of the greatest creative works ever made come from the Anime Industry.

But over the years, the binge-watching excitement slowly faded, and I shifted to being a weekly viewer. I kept looking for that same high again.

That search led me to Solo Leveling and Vagabond, my first manhwa and manga.
Then I discovered Omniscient Reader’s Viewpoint and that changed everything.

With Omniscient Reader’s Viewpoint, I couldn’t wait.
After hitting a cliff-hanger, I immediately switched to the web novel.
I had no idea so many of my favorite anime and manhwa were originally adapted from novels.

I had only read non-fiction out of curiosity, but ORV pulled me in even though I was using a very basic built-in TTS voice.

Then I discovered audiobooks on YouTube. There weren’t many good ones for ORV, so I tried Shadow Slave first because it was highly rated on the novel site I used.

I instantly got attached to Sunny as a character the same way I once rooted for Naruto. The depth of storytelling blew me away.

And then it happened… The channel got banned.

Not just the novel I was following but multiple playlists I wanted to explore someday. I genuinely felt depressed that day. I searched everywhere but couldn’t find a narrator or TTS voice that felt right.

That’s when a thought hit me
Why not create my own audiobook?

I researched different tools. Most good TTS systems were paid, but ElevenLabs had a usable free tier. I decided to test it by narrating Reverend Insanity because of the hype. I recorded the files and saved them.

Later, I faced storage issues and thought about deleting them. But instead, I decided to upload them to YouTube simply as a backup.

And that one small decision created this channel.

Novel WaveHz didn’t start as a YouTube dream. It started as a place to store files.

But it eventually helped me stay consistent, enjoy the novels, and share the stories I love.

That’s how this journey began.
And if you’re here reading this. Thank you for being part of it. This channel exists because of a banned audiobook.
